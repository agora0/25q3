MiniStory: Ha Ha Ha!

(Mr. Stick) noticed the recent popularity of the word " HA ! "

Whether at work, chatting with online friends, or shopping online, it's everywhere.

For example: 

"You're welcome, HA ! " 

"Can minimize it, HA ! " 

"Adjust it a bit, HA ! "

"Need to rush to work overtime, HA ! "

(Mr. Stick) didn't know when this usage became popular, didn't understand its meaning, and felt it sounds bad.

But that's how internet slang is; you might not like how it sounds, but it becomes popular anyway, and then you find yourself saying it too.

(Stick) asked some people, but no one could give him a correct answer.

Maybe it's a dialect, maybe it's colloquial, could it be Thai, Japanese, or Korean?

"Remember to give us a review, ha ! " 

"Today is his birthday, ha ! " 

"These are the sizes, ha ! "

Stick guessed that this "ha" was similar in meaning to "ah," "neh," "ya," "wa," "yo," "sai."

Just some interjections or something to add to the atmosphere.

Out of curiosity, Stick started using this " ha ! " at the end of his sentences when chatting with others.

However, no one seemed to criticize his usage.

One day, an online friend he had known for two years urgently contacted him.

His live-streaming sales channel was incredibly busy.

He asked Stick to do some outsourcing work.

And Stick's income had been unlucky recently, so he wanted to understand more about it.

Actually, the job was very simple.

First, add a "ha" to the end of all the automated messages.

You understand! You've said quite a few already, ha!

In addition, you just need to laugh twice after the streamer speaks, you just "ha ha ha!"

Stick asked: "Just 'ha ha ha' ? "

The online friend said: "Yes! Just 'ha ha ha' ."

Stick quickly realized what the other person needed, he said: "Is it like the supporting character next to the host on a TV variety show?

Just say: "Really?" "Yeah, right?" "Hahaha!"

Never a complete sentence, is that right?"

The online friend said: "Yes, yes, yes! You're so smart.

No problem starting work right away, okay?

Ah....! What should I call you?"

Mr. Stick said: "It's all the same, ha... "

"just call me 'Ha ha ha' ! "


End
